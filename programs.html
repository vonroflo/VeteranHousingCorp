Our Programs

Five pathways from crisis to stability. Whatever a veteran needs — tonight, this month, this year — we have a program built around them.

Tonight

Emergency Housing

For veterans in acute crisis — sleeping outside, fleeing unsafe situations, or facing imminent loss of shelter. We move fast.

Who it’s for

  • Veterans currently unsheltered or about to be
  • Veterans escaping unsafe domestic situations
  • Veterans discharged from medical or correctional settings without housing

What we provide on day one

  • A bed, a hot meal, and a locked door
  • An immediate intake conversation — no judgment, no paperwork barriers
  • Referrals to VA medical and benefits services

Short-term

Temporary Housing

A stable place to stay while we help you build the next step. Bridge housing for veterans actively working toward permanent stability.

Duration

  • Typically 30–90 days, depending on individual circumstances
  • Extensions reviewed case-by-case

Services included

  • Furnished room or unit
  • Weekly check-ins with a case manager
  • Transition planning toward transitional or permanent housing

90–180 days

Transitional Housing

A structured 90–180 day program with case management, VA partnership services, and a clear plan toward independent living.

What it includes

  • Wraparound case management — mental health, employment, benefits navigation
  • Skills-building: budgeting, tenancy readiness, job-search support
  • VA partnership services and direct VA referrals
  • Clear, written transition plan with regular milestones

Success metrics

  • Veterans leaving the program with permanent housing in hand
  • Stable income or income plan
  • Connection to long-term VA care

30%–140% AMI

Affordable Housing

Income-qualified, dignified, long-term affordable units for veterans and their families across a wide income range.

AMI qualification tiers

  • 30% AMI — extremely low income
  • 50% AMI — very low income
  • 80% AMI — low income
  • 120% AMI — workforce
  • 140% AMI — workforce-plus

Application process

  • Submit interest via the contact form or phone
  • Income verification and eligibility review
  • Waitlist placement when units are not immediately available

Built for Generations.

From income-qualified families to lifelong affordability — a model designed to outlast any single grant cycle.

Forever

Permanent Affordability

A model designed to last beyond any single grant cycle, market shift, or political moment. We are creating perpetual affordable housing for individuals and families.

Our long-term mission

  • Acquire and steward properties dedicated to permanent affordability
  • Build a portfolio that outlasts founders, board members, and economic cycles
  • Treat affordability not as a phase but as a permanent commitment

Community impact

  • Stable veterans become stable neighbors, employees, and mentors
  • Generational impact — children grow up housed, not in shelters
  • Marion County and surrounding communities are stronger for it
